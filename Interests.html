Interests

You See Yourself in The Strangest of Places

When I was about 17 years old, a goal I accomplished involved building and programming my first computer completely from researching parts. I was already heavily invested in learning about technology from a young start, but when I was 17, I had my first job and saved the money to buy all the pieces. I would say that I have been in love with technology since.

Life changes and if you look away to long you'll miss it

The most significant motivation to accomplish my goals comes from the deep-ridden negativity that has hindered my success in the past. I spent a lot of my life listening to the people that shot down every idea that I had and any idea of what I wanted to do in life. It took a long time to realize they either thought what I wanted was out of my reach or they couldn't do it themselves, so someone else shouldn't either. Regardless of their intentions, it put me in a pretty dark place for quite a while, and I got caught up in the wheel. I went around and around with those thoughts, and they didn't get me anywhere closer to where I wanted to be or what I aspire to accomplish. I think it only confused me on what I am capable of doing. Once I started listening to myself and figuring out where I wanted to go gave me the insight to see my mistakes, not to fault them but to learn from them.

So.... Why not just learn anything I can about everything that interests me? That's my plan.

Less Stress
I enjoy deciding when to complete specific actions and when certain projects would work out based on deadlines further out rather than more closely monitored projects.
Preferred Work
I prefer a job that I primarily work work independently, but I would like to be in a situation where I regularly work with clients. Either in media production, filming, photography, or coding.
Dreaming Forward
I want to create valuable and meaningful things either for people to use daily or consume daily. This work is either through creating multi-media or through the work I do programming. I want to give back in a way that doesn't involve closing minds but rather opening them to something different or enjoyable.
Less Mundane
I prefer a little consistency in my work, but the content of every day could be entirely different. I feel that I enjoy having the option to alternate on what I need to accomplish, but the things I do need to perform are ever-changing is critical to keep my interests moving forward. I, too often, get caught in the mundane of repetition and lose interest, so involving myself in familiar motions with ever-changing content I believe is critical to my success.
Future Thoughts
Ten years into the future, I am looking to take the time to work when it suits my life schedule. That could mean I work a few months out of the year and take a few months off work depending on contract work and self-funded or investor-funded ventures. I am not looking to have the work; I do feel like it is work.
